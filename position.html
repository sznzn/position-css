Position Properties Explained

Static: Default position. Elements follow the normal document flow. When other elements (absolute/fixed) are removed from the flow, static elements will move up to fill the empty space.

Relative: Positioned relative to its normal position. The element's original space is preserved in the document flow, and other elements will not be affected by its movement.

Absolute: Positioned relative to the nearest positioned ancestor (parent with position: relative/absolute/fixed). The element is completely removed from the normal document flow, allowing other elements to take its original space.

Fixed: Positioned relative to the viewport (browser window). The element is removed from the document flow and stays in the same position even when scrolling. Other elements will move up to fill its original space.

Movement Details:

- Relative (Blue box): Moves 20px from top and 20px from left from its original position

- Absolute (Red box): Moves 50px from top and 50px from left relative to its positioned parent

- Fixed (Green box): Positioned 20px from bottom and 20px from right of the viewport
